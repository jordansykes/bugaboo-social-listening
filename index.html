🚨 Active Recall Issued May 2026 · UK Office for Product Safety & Standards · Fix expected end of June 2026 (~2–3 weeks)
Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapter Set
Product ref: 2605-0189 · Serial numbers starting NL311- · Sold June 2025 – May 2026
Affected brand
Joolz
Sister brand · Bugaboo Group
Safety risk
High
Affected products
All NL311- units
Resolution
Refund + revised adapter ~end-Jun
What happened: The Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapter Set may fail to properly engage and lock with the spring-loaded pin on the Aer2 stroller chassis. The adapter can appear secure but may be incorrectly locked ("false lock") — if it detaches during use, the car seat can tip forward and fall to the ground with the infant inside.
Affected products: All Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapter Sets with serial numbers beginning NL311-, sold between June 2025 and May 2026. The Joolz Aer2 stroller itself is not affected and can continue to be used safely without the adapter.
Consumer action: Stop using the adapter immediately — even if it appears properly attached. Customers will receive a refund email with a form link and prepaid return label. A revised, corrected adapter version is expected at the end of June 2026 (~3 weeks from now).

What happened: The screws securing the front legs of the Bugaboo Giraffe highchair can loosen or fall out during normal use, allowing the front legs to fully detach. 22 reports of detachment, 13 minor injuries. Most likely to occur when a child pushes on the footrest.
Class action dispute: Sturtz v. Bugaboo North America Inc., Case No. 1:25-cv-05688, SDNY — alleges recall notice unfairly blamed consumers ("screws not fully tightened during setup"). Bugaboo offering free repair kit but no refunds. No settlement announced as of 9 Jun 2026.
Stroller range not affected: This recall is specific to the Giraffe highchair. All Bugaboo strollers — Fox 5, Donkey 6, Butterfly 2, Dragonfly, Bee, Ant, Kangaroo — are unaffected.
