What happened: The Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapter Set may fail to properly engage and lock with the spring-loaded pin on the Aer2 stroller chassis — a "false lock" where the adapter appears secure but may detach, causing the car seat to tip forward and fall with the infant inside. US: 1 incident reported, no injuries. Global: 28 incidents, 2 injuries.
Affected products: All Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapter Sets with serial numbers beginning NL311-, sold between June 2025 and May 2026 at Bloomingdale's, Nordstrom, and online. The Joolz Aer2 stroller itself is not affected.
Status update (6 Jul 2026): Joolz publicly committed to shipping the revised, corrected Aer2 Car Seat Adapter Set by "end of June 2026," with affected customers to receive an email notification. As of this week, no shipping notification, customer email, or updated timeline has been identified in public sources — the commitment is now a full week overdue. Refund process via prepaid return label remains active in the interim. What happened: The screws securing the front legs of the Bugaboo Giraffe highchair can loosen or fall out during normal use, allowing the front legs to fully detach. 22 reports of detachment, 13 minor injuries. Most likely to occur when a child pushes on the footrest.
Class action: Sturtz v. Bugaboo North America Inc., Case No. 1:25-cv-05688, SDNY — alleges recall notice unfairly blamed consumers ("screws not fully tightened during setup"). Bugaboo offering free repair kit but no refunds. No settlement announced as of 6 Jul 2026.
Stroller range not affected: This recall is specific to the Giraffe highchair. All Bugaboo strollers — Fox 5 Renew, Donkey 6, Butterfly 2, Dragonfly, Bee, Ant, Kangaroo — are unaffected and clear of any recall or safety action.
